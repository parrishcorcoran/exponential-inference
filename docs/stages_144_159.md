# Stages 144–159 — Mac Session 2026-04-26

Backfilled formal log. These were run on Mac MPS, Qwen3-0.6B vanilla, in the
session preceding Stage 160 (shape fine-tune). Listed in run order.

## Stage 144 — KV-Medusa replication on Qwen3-0.6B

**Hypothesis:** Strix's 14B finding (cos_k 0.74–0.81 uniform across 10 offsets,
no decay) is a structural property of K-prediction, not a 14B-specific artifact
or KV-256 substrate effect.

**Method:** Train 10 KV-Medusa heads, one per offset (300 steps each), MSE loss
on (predicted_K, real_K) and (predicted_V, real_V) at layer 14. Same recipe as
Strix's `pipeline_kv_medusa.py`, scaled to 0.6B.

**Result:** cos_k 0.71–0.77 across all 10 offsets — uniform, no decay.
cos_v 0.23–0.41 (V uniformly weaker — same K/V asymmetry as 14B).

**Lesson:** K-uniformity is scale-stable. The HRR address-book structure
predicted by Plate (1995) is present at both 0.6B and 14B.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b.py`, `results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b.json`

## Stage 145 — KV-substitution token-acceptance test (oracle drafts)

**Hypothesis:** Predicted KV at cos ≈ 0.75 is functionally interchangeable
with real KV — the model produces the same top-1 token when its cache is
substituted with our predictions.

**Method:** For each anchor t, predict K, V at positions t+1..t+10 from h_t.
Substitute into layer-14 cache. Run forward with the *same baseline tokens*
as input (oracle drafts). Compare top-1 to baseline.

**Result:** **96.0% top-1 acceptance** across all 10 offsets, 50 anchors per
offset. Strix's 14B got 99.1%; the 3-pt gap matches the cos_k gap (0.81 vs 0.77).

**Lesson:** Predicted K, V is *operationally* a correct address book.
Speedup pipeline's *cache half* works. Wallclock projected separately (Stage 146).

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_token_test.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_token_test.json`

## Stage 146 — Wallclock benchmark on Mac MPS

**Hypothesis:** Speedup ≈ tokens-per-step / verify-step-time.

**Method:** Measure baseline greedy tok/s, head inference time, verify-pass
time on a length-21 sequence. Compute projected Medusa tok/s.

**Result:**
- Baseline greedy: 27.28 tok/s (36.65 ms/tok).
- 10 heads inference: 2.12 ms.
- Verify forward pass: 55.70 ms.
- Projected speedup: **5.55× over baseline** assuming 8.76 tokens/step.

**Lesson:** With oracle drafts, the architecture is sound. The bottleneck
becomes drafting, not cache prediction.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_speed.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_speed.json`

## Stage 147 — Standard token-Medusa heads on 0.6B

**Hypothesis:** Standard Medusa (each head predicts a token at offset +k from
h_t directly) provides drafts. Quality should mirror published Medusa-1.

**Method:** Train 5 heads, 300 steps each, CE loss against actual tokens.

**Result:** Validation accuracy **0.318 / 0.052 / 0.025 / 0.026 / 0.019** at
offsets 1–5. Chained ≈ 1.34 tokens/step.

**Lesson:** Standard Medusa is too weak at 0.6B for spec decoding to clear
1× speedup. The tokens-per-step is binding.

`scripts/pipeline_medusa_heads_06b.py`,

## Stage 148 — Combined token-Medusa + KV-Medusa acceptance

**Hypothesis:** Adding KV-Medusa cache substitution to standard-Medusa
drafts boosts spec-decode acceptance by removing cache-cascade errors.

**Method:** Token-Medusa heads draft 5 tokens; KV-Medusa heads predict K, V
for those positions; verify with substituted cache.

**Result:** Per-offset acceptance **identical with vs without** KV substitution
(0.84 / 0.14 / 0.04 / 0.06 / 0.10 in both cases).

**Lesson:** KV-Medusa cannot rescue wrong drafts. The wrong drafted token
makes Q at that position wrong; even a perfect K, V cache can't fix a wrong
probe. **The Q-bottleneck.**

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_combined.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_combined.json`

## Stage 149 — Tree-Medusa top-K probe

**Hypothesis:** Branching K candidates per offset (top-K instead of top-1)
should significantly raise the chained acceptance ceiling.

**Method:** For K ∈ {1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64}, measure per-offset top-K hit rate
(is the model's natural top-1 in head-k's top-K candidates?). Compute chained
1 + a₁·a₂·… ceiling under independence assumption.

**Result:**
| K | chained tokens/step |
|---|---------------------|
| 1 | 1.96 |
| 8 | 2.56 |
| 32 | 2.91 |
| 64 | 3.16 |

**Lesson:** Tree expansion tops out at ~3.16 tokens/step on 0.6B even with
huge K. The model's intrinsic top-K mass at deep offsets is the limit.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_tree.py`,

## Stage 150 — Conditional KV-Medusa (head conditioned on candidate token)

**Hypothesis:** A KV-Medusa head that takes (h_t, embed(token_at_offset)) as
input can predict K, V *conditional on which token is being placed there*,
which would let it adapt to draft tokens that differ from baseline.

**Method:** Same architecture as KV-Medusa heads, but input doubled to include
candidate token embedding. Train 5 heads, 300 steps each.

**Result:** Cosine improvements: cos_k +0.002 → +0.009 across offsets (small),
cos_v +0.005 → +0.041 (growing with offset).

**Lesson:** Conditioning adds modest signal at the cosine level. Token
acceptance test in Stage 151 will show whether it composes.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_conditional.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_conditional.json`

## Stage 151 — Combined cond-KV vs uncond-KV vs no-sub

**Hypothesis:** Conditional KV-Medusa should beat unconditional in the
combined setup (token-Medusa drafts + KV-Medusa cache sub).

**Method:** Run the Stage 148 pipeline with three KV configurations:
no substitution, unconditional, conditional.

**Result:** Per-offset acceptance identical in all three cases.
Chained 1.96 / 1.97 / 1.97 — **conditional adds zero token-level gain.**

**Lesson:** The Q-path corruption (Stage 148 diagnosis) is the bottleneck;
no clever K-conditioning fixes it. KV-Medusa cannot help wrong drafts at all.

## Stage 152 — Eagle drafter training

**Hypothesis:** Autoregressive drafting (Eagle: predict h at future positions
recurrently) gives higher token quality than parallel Medusa heads.

**Method:** 1-layer transformer drafter, MSE on hidden state + CE through
LM head. 1500 steps on Mac MPS.

**Result:** val_tok_acc **0.148** at offset 1 — undertrained. Eagle paper uses
50K+ steps with batch=8.

**Lesson:** Eagle architecture is right but Mac-MPS budget is too small for
full convergence. Result is a baseline, not a competitive drafter.

## Stage 153 — Eagle autoregressive test

**Hypothesis:** Even an undertrained Eagle has autoregressive context that
helps deep offsets relative to standard Medusa's parallel prediction.

**Method:** Run autoregressive Eagle drafting at offsets 1..5. Measure
verify-pass acceptance against baseline.

**Result:** Eagle accept 0.32 / 0.20 / 0.18 / 0.22 / 0.18 — beats standard
Medusa at offsets 2–5 (autoregressive context helps), loses overall on
chained tokens-per-step (1.40 vs 1.96 for standard Medusa K=1).

**Lesson:** Eagle's mechanism is right; quality scales with training.
Undertrained Eagle drifts after offset 3. Confirmed: the architecture isn't
broken, just hungry for compute.

## Stage 154 — K-decoder v1 (real-K → token via frozen LM head)

**Hypothesis:** K alone contains substantial token information; a small
linear projection from K to d_model, plus the frozen LM head, recovers the
token at that position.

**Method:** 1M-param Linear(d_kv → d_model). Train 1000 steps on (real_K, token)
pairs. Eval on val.

**Result:** **Real K → token top-1 = 0.560.** K alone carries over half the
token signature. Predicted-K (cos~0.75) collapses to 0.12 top-1 — too noisy.

**Lesson:** The structural HRR claim holds — K is the unbinding key. But
predicted-K at cos 0.75 lands outside the cleanup-memory basins.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_unbind.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_unbind.json`

## Stage 155 — K-decoder v2 (noise-augmented training)

**Hypothesis:** Train the decoder on *predicted* K from KV-Medusa (the noisy
distribution it'll see at inference), not on real K. Should produce a
noise-robust readout.

**Method:** 1500 steps, decoder reads K from KV-Medusa head outputs (random
offset per step). Wider 2-layer MLP for capacity.

**Result:** Real-K ceiling collapsed to **0.096** (was 0.560 in v1). Noise
during training destroyed the decoder's discrimination — coarse mapping that
handles noise but loses signal.

**Lesson:** Training-on-predicted alone doesn't work. The head and decoder
need to *co-adapt*, not the decoder alone. Sets up Stage 156.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_unbind_v2.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_unbind_v2.json`

## Stage 156 — Joint focused 1×1 training (head + decoder, MSE+CE)

**Hypothesis:** One head, one decoder, one offset, joint MSE+CE loss. The
head and decoder co-adapt so the head's predicted K lands in the decoder's
basins.

**Method:** Warm-start head from Stage 144's offset-1 head and decoder from
Stage 154's v1. Joint training 2000 steps with MSE on (K, V) + CE on
decoder(K_pred) → token.

**Result:** **Predicted-K → token top-1 = 0.210, top-5 = 0.460.** First
breakthrough on predicted K. cos_k stayed at 0.755 (head didn't have to
abandon the K manifold to become decodable).

**Lesson:** Joint training is the methodology. Cosine alignment + readout
co-adapt to find a token-decodable region within the K manifold.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_joint_one.py`,
`results/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_joint_one.json`

## Stage 157 — Anneal head 2 with frozen head-1 anchor

**Hypothesis:** Annealing forward (train offset 2 with offset 1 frozen) extends
the joint methodology to multiple offsets without regression.

**Method:** Freeze head 1 from Stage 156. Initialize head 2 from head 1.
Train 1500 steps with shared decoder.

**Result:** Offset 1 preserved (0.220 / 0.460). Offset 2 reached **0.090 / 0.240**
top-1/top-5 — significantly weaker than offset 1.

**Lesson:** Anchor annealing preserves prior offsets. Deep offsets are
fundamentally harder because h_t carries less info about further-out tokens.
Shared decoder is also a soft compromise; per-offset decoders may help (untested).

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_anneal_h2.py`,

## Stage 158 — Q-only decoder ceiling

**Hypothesis:** Q is structurally a different probe than K (queries vs
addresses in HRR). Q may carry more or different token information.

**Method:** Capture Q at layer 14 via attention forward hook, train a
1M-param Linear projection Q → d_model → frozen LM head, measure ceiling.

**Result:** **Real-Q top-1 = 0.615, top-5 = 0.785.** Q is *more informative*
than K (0.560 top-1).

**Lesson:** The probe (Q) carries more token-conditional information than the
address (K) at this layer. Combined K + V + Q decoder is a natural next move.

`scripts/pipeline_kv_medusa_06b_q_only.py`,

## Stage 159 — Layer sweep + decoder-as-LM + Medusa-5-as-LM + Jacobi tests

**Hypothesis bundle:** (a) the optimal layer for K, V, Q prediction is somewhere
in the middle; (b) using decoders as language models (no verify) produces
coherent text; (c) Jacobi parallel decoding converges to greedy fixed-point and
provides speedup if init is good.

**Method:** 28-layer probe sweep with multi-output triple head. Then tests of:
decoder-as-LM (use K+V+Q decoder as next-token producer); Medusa-5-as-LM
(stack 5 head argmaxes); Jacobi parallel decode with placeholder, Medusa,
and Eagle inits.

**Results:**
- **Layer sweep:** K and Q peak at L15 (cos 0.79 / 0.46), V peaks at L16
  (cos 0.45). Other-Claude's closed-form linear probe later confirmed L14 is
  optimal under linear regression and dual-layer probes don't help (Finding 23).
- **Decoder-as-LM:** PPL 22 (vs baseline 2.9, ~7× higher) — English-shaped
  but topically drifts.
- **Medusa-5-as-LM:** PPL 8 numerically but qualitatively degenerate
  ("the the the the the" loops).
- **Jacobi parallel decode:** Converges to greedy (text-identical), but at
  0.83–0.94× speedup on 0.6B — drafters too weak to give Jacobi a useful
  init.

**Lesson:** Layer 14–16 is the right address-book region. Pure decoder-as-LM
is incoherent at our quality level. Jacobi + good drafter is the right
architecture but Mac-trained drafters aren't strong enough. The *base-model*
needs reshaping (sets up Stage 160).

## Aggregate session lesson

Stages 144–159 measured the **structural** properties of Qwen3-0.6B's K, V, Q
manifolds and confirmed they support HRR-style retrieval (real-K 56% / real-Q
62% top-1 token decode). They also established the **dynamic** limit: with the
*stock* model, predicted-K → token tops out at 21% (joint focused, Stage 156)
and Jacobi+drafter cannot beat 1× speedup (Stage 159).

**The diagnosis at the end of Stage 159:** the model itself isn't shaped for
KV-prediction — it produces a cache that's good for attention but not for
external readout. The fix is to *train* the model to be KV-prediction-friendly.
That's Stage 160 (shape fine-tune), where cos_k jumped from 0.77 → 0.91 and
KV-substitution acceptance jumped from 96% → 100%.
