## The project in one sentence

Transformer hidden states live on a low-dim (~10) curved manifold
that's a property of the tokenizer family. Every LLM is an imperfect
approximation to this manifold. Generation is a geodesic traversal on
its surface. Compute can be compressed on the boundary but not in the
bulk. **A small model trained against the measured manifold — not
against a teacher's approximation of it — may exceed any teacher; or
more usefully, can serve as a high-confidence speculative-decoding
drafter.**

## The big result from today (2026-04-21)

Stage 54d: a 15M-param student, trained from scratch on 150k
wikitext-2 tokens for 4000 steps (8.5 minutes on Mac MPS), using
contrastive softmax over rank-64 projected embeddings as the training
target — NO teacher logits, NO teacher hidden states in the loss —
achieves:

- Top-10% most-confident predictions: **0.400 agreement with Qwen3-0.6B teacher**
- Top-20% most-confident: 0.233 agreement
- Top-30% most-confident: 0.189 agreement
- All predictions: 0.117 agreement

The student is 67× smaller than the teacher. The speculative-decoding
acceptance rate at the confident tail is already in useful territory
at this tiny scale.

This validates Theory #6's pipeline end-to-end: training against pure
manifold coordinates (projected-embedding geometry, no teacher in the
loss) produces a model whose confident predictions are
speculative-decoding-useful. Scaling is the remaining question.

## Parrish's measurement-limit insight (2026-04-21)

Critical correction to how we evaluate Theory #6:

**Teacher-agreement is only a valid quality metric while the teacher is
the ground truth.** Past a certain scale, the student's manifold
representation becomes more precise than the teacher's. At that point,
teacher-agreement PEAKS and then DECLINES as the student surpasses
teacher resolution — because the student starts predicting things the
teacher can't yet resolve.

The correct metrics beyond this point:

1. **Perplexity on held-out corpus** (corpus is ground truth)
2. **Multi-teacher ensemble consensus** (average is closer to truth than any single teacher)
3. **Divergence signature**: plot `(student_ppl − teacher_ppl)` over training. If it goes negative, student has exceeded teacher on the corpus.

For speculative decoding specifically, the important metric is
**confidence-stratified agreement**, not overall accuracy. Student
doesn't need to exceed teacher accuracy; it needs high agreement on
high-confidence predictions for high acceptance rate.

## The stage 54 series — what we built today

Four scripts building up the manifold-target training pipeline:

- **54** (cosine loss, tiny hardcoded corpus): converged train, failed generalization
- **54b** (contrastive loss): NaN on MPS from SDPA/normalize bugs
- **54c** (after fixes: manual attention, manual normalize, wikitext-2 data): works, reached 30% of teacher accuracy at 9M params
- **54d** (scaled to 15M, 150k tokens, 4000 steps, checkpoint eval every 500 steps with perplexity + confidence-stratified agreement): 40% agreement at top-10% student confidence

The MPS-specific fixes that unblocked training:
- Replace `F.scaled_dot_product_attention` with manual matmul+softmax+matmul
- Replace `F.normalize(x)` with `x / x.norm(keepdim=True).clamp_min(eps)`
- Keep fp32 throughout the trainable path; only embedding lookup reads bf16

## Z8G4 can train without a teacher

Important realization: the stage 54 training loop doesn't use the
teacher except for its embedding matrix (~310MB, trivial). The
teacher's forward pass is NEVER in the training loop. The teacher is
only loaded for EVAL comparison.

Z8 can therefore run stage 54d on CPU with:
- Just the tokenizer + embedding matrix
- PCA basis construction
- Small student training on CPU (tiny relative to GPU, but 700 GB RAM is plenty)
- Multi-teacher ensemble (average embeddings from multiple sizes → richer manifold)

Z8's niche becomes: **manifold-ensemble measurement + teacher-free
training at very large corpus scale**. Slow per step but unattended
overnight produces large training runs.

## Important gotchas for a new Claude

All the gotchas from 2026-04-20_handoff.md still apply:

1. Physics-first framing — don't revert to standard ML when surprised
2. "Match" always means teacher-vs-student, NOT self-vs-self-at-k_max
3. "Coherent" is not a number — show token-match or side-by-side samples
4. User rejects hybrid framings — look for the unifying principle
5. Don't trust commit messages without checking the code
6. Bulk compression always fails (Finding 10)
7. Manifold floor ~80-160M is real; 0.6B failures often sub-floor
8. MPS hook/kernel overhead; ROCm handles same code better
9. KV is a boundary object, separable from heads/width/length
10. Frames that survived: RG flow (Finding 11), quantum measurement. Dead: fractal, CFT scaling, RSB glass, parallel transport.

**New gotchas added today:**

11. Teacher-agreement is only valid below teacher resolution. Track
    perplexity + multi-teacher ensemble + divergence signature for
    quality past that limit.

12. Small student models need real data (wikitext-2 or bigger). Hand-
    coded 20-text calibration corpora won't generalize — 300 training
    pairs just memorize.

13. On MPS, avoid `F.scaled_dot_product_attention`'s backward, avoid
    `F.normalize` — they produce NaN grads. Use manual attention and
    manual clamp-normalize. Stage 54b→54c in commit history has the
    fix.

14. Teacher-free training is possible. Training loop only needs the
    embedding matrix + corpus. Teacher forward passes are only for
    EVAL comparison. This makes Z8G4 a viable student-training
    machine.
